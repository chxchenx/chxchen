Essigmann Lab, MIT

undergraduate researcher, data analysis

September 2019 - current

The Essigmann Lab at MIT's Department of Biological Engineering studies DNA lesions and mutations caused by DNA damaging agents, focusing on mutation, cancer, and cell death.

During my time with the lab, I've:
  • developed software to efficiently process, analyze, and visualize mutational data
  • ran Monte Carlo simulations to advise research decisions
  • used the Biopython package to analyze mutations in the SARS-Cov2 (COVID-19) virus to investigate mechanisms of viral evolution
